## Findings / caveats

- **GPQA:** all three QAD students land in 0.540–0.551, i.e. clearly above the naive NVFP4 from 001 (0.505) and below bf16 (0.581). QAD (quantization-aware distillation) recovers a meaningful chunk of the reasoning lost to plain NVFP4. Best GPQA: `w4a16-kvfp8-cast` (0.5505).
- **RULER (13-task mean):** the three are close (0.761–0.777), all near the 001 NVFP4 level (0.775) and a bit under bf16 (0.795). `w4a16-kvfp8-cast` is highest here too (0.777).
- **`ruler_vt` = 0.000 for every model** (here and in 001). Consistent across bf16/NVFP4/all QAD variants ⇒ a systematic ruler_vt behaviour under non-thinking Qwen3-8B (answer format / variable-tracking), not a quant effect. Kept in the 13-task mean (reported means never exclude subtasks).
- **`niah_multikey_2`** is the other consistently low task (0.18–0.40 across models) — task difficulty, not a quant delta.
- All 42 jobs reached their intended totals with 0 errored trials (verified against the eval service).
